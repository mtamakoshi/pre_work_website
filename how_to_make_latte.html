How to Draw a Rosetta

1

Get Ready

"Conditioning" your milk right before pouring it. "conditioning" means swirl it around the pitcher so that it stays shiny on top instead of the matte look that indicates that milk and form have started separating.

2

High and Slow

Start by pouring a think stream of milk from about 3 inches above the top rim of the mug. The thin, liquidy milk will sink below the foamy coffee and create a supporting base for it in the bowl of the mug.

3

Bring it Down Low

After you've pirece the crema, continue to pour slowly until the mug is about 1/3 full.Then, steadly bring your pitcher down all the way, resting it against the cup as you continue to pour.

4

The Dot

When you birng the pitcher down low and increase your flow, you should see a dot of white foam collec on the top of the coffee. Then you should star to moving the pitcher from side to side at this point. (Don't lif the pitche yet!!)

5

Keep Moving

Continue to gently rock the pticher side to side using the "pumping" action from the last slide while increasing the volume of your pour.

6

Top of the Design

Once you reach the far end of the cup and the top of your rosetta, start pouring a little less milk volmue out of your pitcher.

7

Pour Through

To turn the wave into petal, life the pitcher (Finally!) off the side of the cup and back up on your pour a little, while moving the milk stream forward, toward your thumb and the wider "leaves".

8

Let's sip

The greatest thing about latte art might be the face that once you learn how to do it, it comes as naturally as drinking the result.
